Their Story

She Didn't Choose This Life.
But You Can Change It.

Imagine being five years old, standing before a magistrate's court — not because you did something wrong, but because the world failed you. No mother. No father. Nowhere safe to sleep.

The girls who come to Vishaka arrive through Sri Lanka's court system — orphaned by loss, abandoned by circumstance, or removed from homes where abuse and neglect had stolen their childhood. They arrive frightened, often traumatised, carrying wounds invisible to the eye.

Since 1952, Vishaka Child Development Centre in Kalutara has been their sanctuary. More than just a shelter — it is a family. A dedicated resident matron becomes their "Amma" (mother). Teachers support them through school. Counsellors tend to their invisible scars. And slowly, one day at a time, they begin to heal.

Many of these girls go on to sit their A/Level examinations, enter university, and become independent, compassionate citizens. But none of it is possible without continuous support — food, clothes, sanitary needs, books, and above all, a safe roof overhead.

❝ "Every child that walks through our gates deserves the chance to become the best version of herself." — Vishaka Child Development Centre

Daily Care

What Your Donation
Makes Possible

Every contribution — large or small — directly funds the everyday essentials that turn a frightened child into a thriving young woman.

🍚

Nutritious Meals

Three wholesome, balanced meals every day. For many girls, Vishaka is the first place they've ever known food security. Your donation keeps plates full.

📚

Education & Books

All girls attend recognised government schools, supported through GCE A/Levels and beyond. We supply stationery, uniforms, school fees, and after-school tutoring.

🧴

Sanitary & Personal Care

Hygiene products, sanitary essentials, and personal care items — needs that are often overlooked but profoundly important to a young girl's dignity and well-being.

👗

Clothing

Clean, age-appropriate clothing so every girl can present herself with confidence at school and in society, free from the shame poverty imposes.

💊

Healthcare

Regular medical check-ups, dental care, and mental health support to heal the physical and psychological wounds that brought them here.

💝

Love & Family

A devoted "Amma" (house mother) in every home, workshops, life skills training, and the most powerful gift of all — the knowledge that someone cares.

Building Campaign

Five Homes.
Sixty Girls.
One Dream.

The existing buildings at Vishaka are old — over 70 years of service have taken their toll. Dark corridors, insufficient light and ventilation, and no spaces designed with a child's spirit in mind. Our girls deserve better.

We are building the Vishaka Balika Niwasaya (Girls' Village) — five purpose-built homes, each housing 12 girls with their own devoted house mother. Inspired by Sinhala Buddhist values, each home will offer private bedrooms, a study area, a kitchen, outdoor verandahs, and spaces for play, reflection, and growth.
